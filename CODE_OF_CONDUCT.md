# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to making participation in this project respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, or ability.

## Expected Behavior

Contributors and maintainers should:

- Communicate with empathy, patience, and respect.
- Give constructive feedback focused on the work, not the person.
- Welcome questions and differing technical viewpoints.
- Respect privacy, especially when discussing NYU or Albert data.
- Accept responsibility, apologize when appropriate, and learn from mistakes.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discriminatory language, personal attacks, threats, sexualized content, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, or publishing another person's private information without permission.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other project spaces. It also applies when someone publicly represents the project.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub profile. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive personal information. Maintainers will review reports promptly, protect reporters' privacy where possible, and take action proportional to the circumstances.

Possible actions include a private correction, warning, temporary participation restriction, removal of content, or permanent exclusion from project spaces. Retaliation against someone who reports a concern is prohibited.
